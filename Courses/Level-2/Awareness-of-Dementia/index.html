Highfield Level 2 Award in Awareness of Dementia

Home / Highfield Level 2 Award in Awareness of Dementia

Course Overview

This introductory qualification is designed to improve awareness and understanding of Autistic Spectrum Conditions (ASC). It covers the characteristics of autism, how it can affect people differently, and how best to support individuals in various settings such as schools, workplaces, and care environments.

Who is This Course For?

This course is suitable for:

  • Healthcare assistants
  • Support workers and care staff
  • Family members of individuals with dementia
  • Social workers and voluntary sector workers
  • Anyone seeking a foundation in dementia awareness

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, learners will:

  • Understand what dementia is and its common types (e.g., Alzheimer's, Vascular)
  • Recognize how dementia affects the brain and behavior
  • Learn about person-centered approaches to care
  • Understand how to communicate effectively with someone with dementia
  • Explore the importance of inclusion, dignity, and respect in dementia care

Duration & Entry Requirements

Duration: 8–12 weeks (flexible)

Requirements: No formal qualifications. Basic English required.

🔹 Why Take This Course?

  • Build confidence in interacting with people living with dementia
  • Learn the fundamentals of compassionate care
  • Enhance your professional profile in the care industry
  • Fulfills CPD (Continuing Professional Development) requirements

Career Progression

  • Care Assistant
  • Support Worker
  • Further studies – Level 3 Diploma or Apprenticeships
