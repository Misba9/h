Principles of Equality, Diversity and Rights

Home / Principles of Equality, Diversity and Rights

Course Overview

This course provides essential knowledge and awareness of equality, diversity, and rights within health and social care settings. It helps learners understand how to support individuals fairly, respectfully, and without discrimination, promoting an inclusive and person-centered approach to care.

It’s ideal for individuals working—or aspiring to work—in any care, health, or support setting.

Who Should Take This Course?

This course is suitable for:

  • New and experienced health and social care staff
  • Volunteers and support workers
  • Those working with vulnerable adults, children, or people with disabilities
  • Anyone required to comply with the Equality Act 2010

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, learners will:

  • Understand the concepts of equality, diversity, equity, and rights
  • Know how discrimination can occur and how to prevent it
  • Apply inclusive practices in care delivery
  • Understand the importance of promoting individual rights
  • Be aware of relevant legislation and policies
  • Promote dignity, respect, and cultural sensitivity

Duration & Entry Requirements

Duration: 8–12 weeks (flexible)

Requirements: No formal qualifications. Basic English required.

Why Equality & Diversity Training Matters

  • Ensures fair treatment for all individuals
  • Helps meet legal and ethical standards in care
  • Improves quality of life for service users
  • Reduces bias, improves team communication and trust
  • Builds a positive and inclusive culture in the workplace

Career Progression

After completing this course, learners can progress to:

  • Level 2 Certificate in Equality & Diversity
  • Level 2/3 Diploma in Adult Social Care
  • Specialist training in cultural competence or mental health awareness
